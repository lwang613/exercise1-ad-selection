### Key concepts I locked in

- **`bcftools`** is a compiled tool (htslib family) that *streams* variant data record-by-record and pipes into Unix tools (`grep`, `cut`, `awk`, `wc`). Core moves: `view` (select rows/columns), `query` (tidy extract), `+fill-tags` (compute AF).
- **Where tools live:** the executable is at `~/miniforge3/envs/exercise1/bin/bcftools` — inside the conda env, *not* the project repo. Activating the env just puts that `bin/` on my `PATH`.
- **`environment.yml` is a recipe used once** to build the env; `mamba activate` doesn't read it — it goes straight to the pre-built env. The `.yml` then just lives in Git as a record and a way for others to rebuild a twin.
- **Genome builds matter:** the same variant sits at different coordinates on GRCh38 vs GRCh37. Everything here is GRCh38.
- Refreshers: 1000G **super-populations** (AFR/EUR/EAS/SAS/AMR), the **allele → genotype → frequency** chain, and the Unix pipe philosophy.

### What I took from Vitti et al (2013)

- **Two ways to hunt for selection:** *hypothesis-testing* (check specific, pre-chosen traits) vs *hypothesis-generating* (broad genome-wide scans). Positive selection is the practical focus because it is the most conspicuous — purifying selection mostly hides in conserved regions and balancing selection leaves subtle marks.
- **Between species (divergence):** **Ka/Ks** compares non-synonymous to synonymous substitution rates (>1 = positive selection, <1 = purifying, =1 = neutral drift). The **McDonald–Kreitman** test compares that ratio within vs between species; the **HKA** test compares divergence to polymorphism across loci and, unlike MKT, works on *any* region — not just protein-coding.
- **Within species (polymorphism):** a **selective sweep** drags a whole haplotype to fixation and flattens local diversity, distorting the allele-frequency spectrum. **Tajima's D** reads that distortion by comparing two estimates of θ — segregating sites (S) vs average pairwise differences (π). Its sign maps to the shape of the genealogical tree: **negative D = starburst** (rapid expansion *or* a sweep), **positive D = broom** (bottleneck *or* balancing selection). The catch: demography and selection can produce the same sign, so one locus alone can't distinguish them.
- **Specificity:** examining a whole region (many variants) rather than a single site is what separates true local adaptation from genome-wide demographic noise.

### Methods in depth — how `master_table_v1` was built

**Pipeline:** raw supplement (`.xlsx`) → extract Table S2 (CSV) → annotate by joining S2 + S7 (CSV) → select pilot 10 + freeze master table. Three scripts, each doing one job so the whole thing re-runs from raw data.

#### 1. `scripts/01_extract_locus_table.py` — get Table S2 out of Excel
- Opens the raw workbook, sheet `Table S2`.
- The sheet has title/description rows above the data, so the script scans downward for the header row (first cell == `Locus name`) rather than assuming row 1.
- Writes header + data → `data/processed/bellenguez2026_lead_variants_TableS2.csv` (109 rows = 91 Tier1 + 18 Tier2).

#### 2. `scripts/02_annotate_loci.py` — join S2 + S7 and derive the columns
1. **Constants:** GWS threshold = 5e-8; file paths.
2. **`parse_p(v)`** — normalizes the mixed-format p-value cells to floats: `NA` → missing, `0` → 0.0, `< 1E-300` → strip the `<` → 1e-300, ordinary numbers → float.
3. **`parse_or(v)`** — pulls the point estimate out of `"3 (2.95-3.05)"` → 3.0 (the token before the `(`).
4. **Read S7's two-row header.** S7 has a merged *banner* row (group titles) sitting over a *sub-header* row. Merged cells read as blank, so I forward-fill the banner so every column knows its group. Then a helper `col(group, subheader)` finds a column *by name* (e.g. `No-biobank meta-analysis` + `P`) — deliberately by name, not a hard-coded index, so a layout change can't silently point at the wrong column.
5. **Build `s7map`** = dict keyed by **index variant** (the coordinate), holding that row's three P-values (main / no-proxy / no-biobank) + mapped gene / consequence / REVEL. Keyed by coordinate, never by name.
6. **Walk each S2 locus** and compute: REF/ALT (split the index variant); effect/other allele (split EA/OA); **risk allele** = EA if OR>1 else OA; look up the lead in `s7map` by index variant → the three P-values + gene/consequence; `robust_noproxy` / `robust_nobiobank` = P < 5e-8; **Origin** = Clinical AD if robust_nobiobank else Proxy; **confidence tier** A/B/C.
7. **Write** `results/loci_annotated.csv` and print a QC summary (unmatched to S7 = none; 54 Clinical AD / 37 Proxy).

#### 3. `scripts/03_build_master_table.py` — select and freeze the pilot 10
1. Curated pilot list = the 10 *exact* S2 locus names in roadmap order (explicit names, to avoid the `CLU` ↔ `cluster` fuzzy-match false hit).
2. `notes_for()` auto-adds flags: rare-variant gene (TREM2/SORL1/ABCA7), exceptional/complex region (APOE, HLA), low-frequency lead (EAF < 5%).
3. Read `loci_annotated.csv`, select the 10, assign `locus_id` (`AD2026_L001`…), map fields into the 28-column master schema, add `source_GWAS`.
4. Write `results/master_table_v1.csv` (frozen v1).

#### Key decisions (and why)
- **Join on the variant coordinate, not the locus name.** A locus has multiple S7 rows (secondary signals); name-matching grabbed a secondary signal and mis-flagged BIN1/SORL1/ABCA7 as Proxy. Coordinate-matching reads the true lead. (Same "trust the coordinate, not the label" lesson as the rsID/VCF case.)
- **Find columns by header name, not index** — robust to layout drift.
- **Derive risk allele and Origin, never assume** — EA is not always the ALT; 5 of the 10 pilot risk alleles are the *other* allele.
- **Single-variant no-biobank P** for Origin → 54/91 (the paper's 56 also counts a joint-P route; the 2-locus gap is expected, not a bug).
- **Freeze as v1** — a versioned, immutable deliverable.

#### Verification

- master table = 10 rows, no missing required values.
- Cross-checked lead vs secondary-signal rows for BIN1/SORL1/ABCA7 — this is where the name-match bug surfaced and was fixed.

### Revision (same day) — align Origin to the paper and add joint-P → `master_table_v2`

After freezing v1 I reworked the annotation so the table matches the paper's own definitions, then re-froze as v2 (v1 kept immutable).

- **Origin now uses the paper's GWS-in-analysis definition**, not my single-variant p-value threshold. A locus is Clinical-AD if *any* of its signals (lead **or** secondary) is identified in the no-biobank analysis — a **locus-level** count. Aggregating S7's `identifying → no-biobank` (YES/NA) flag per locus reproduces the paper's "**56 of 91**" **exactly**.
- **Why my v1 gave 54, not 56:** two loci reach clinical-AD significance only through a *secondary* signal, not their lead, so a lead-only rule missed them. (I also corrected an earlier mistake: the identifier flag *on the lead* gives 35, not 56 — "identifying" means the analysis actually *led* the locus, which is stricter than merely being GWS there.)
- **No-proxy comes out 74 vs the paper's 75** — one borderline locus at the identifier level. It doesn't affect Origin, and I've flagged it as a known 1-locus gap to revisit.
- **Added joint-P columns** (`Pjoint_main/noproxy/nobiobank`) and the `gws_noproxy/gws_nobiobank` flags to both `loci_annotated.csv` and the master table. Joint P is the conditional (multi-SNP) model's p-value, run separately within each analysis; the single-variant `P` is always populated, so joint P only *adds* signal-resolution where a locus has multiple independent hits.
- **Froze `results/master_table_v2.csv`.** All 10 pilot loci are Clinical-AD-robust under the paper's definition.
